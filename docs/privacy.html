Privacy Policy

Last Updated: March 2026

1. Data Collection by Setup Generator

The Linked Lamp project provides a Setup Guide configuration generator. We do not collect, process, or store any of your data. The entire Setup Guide is a static web page executing 100% locally in your web browser. Any credentials you enter (Names, Wi-Fi, HiveMQ/AWS passwords) are used dynamically within your browser to generate your config.json file and Web App URLs, and are never transmitted to our servers.

2. Third-Party Services (Important)

The Linked Lamp relies heavily on third-party MQTT broker services to route messages across the world. While we do not collect your data, the companies hosting these brokers might.

If you use HiveMQ Cloud, AWS IoT Core, or any other cloud MQTT provider, the signals, durations, and connection metadata flowing between your lamps are processed by their servers. By using the Linked Lamp software to connect to a third-party service, you acknowledge and accept their respective Privacy Policies and Terms of Service. We have no affiliation with these entities and no liability for their data practices.

3. Local Storage

The personalized Web App controller (accessed via your unique /my/ URL) utilizes your browser's Local Storage to save UI states (like Signal Presets). This operates entirely offline relative to any centralized "Linked Lamp Server" because there is no such server. It talks directly to your third-party MQTT broker.

4. Open Source Transparency

The source code for the Linked Lamp firmware and web interface is completely open and public under the GNU General Public License v3.0 (GPLv3). You are encouraged to read the code, modify it, and audit it for your own security requirements.
