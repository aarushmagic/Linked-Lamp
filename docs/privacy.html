Linked Lamp

Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 2026

1. Data Collection by Setup Generator

The Linked Lamp project provides a Setup Guide configuration generator. We do not collect, process, or store any of your data. The entire Setup Guide is a static web page executing 100% locally in your web browser. Any credentials you enter (Names, Wi-Fi, MQTT Broker passwords) are used dynamically within your browser to generate your config.json file and Web App URLs, and are never transmitted to our servers.

2. Third-Party Services (Important)

The Linked Lamp relies heavily on third-party services to route messages and notifications across the world. While we do not collect your data, the companies hosting these services might.

If you use HiveMQ Cloud, Adafruit IO, AWS IoT Core, or any other cloud MQTT provider, the signals, durations, and connection metadata flowing between your lamps are processed by their servers. If you enable Away Mode, your push notification tokens and payloads are processed by Google Firebase. By using the Linked Lamp software to connect to a third-party service, you acknowledge and accept their respective Privacy Policies and Terms of Service. We have no affiliation with these entities and no liability for their data practices.

3. Website Hosting & Analytics

The Linked Lamp documentation and setup tools are hosted via GitHub Pages and routed through Cloudflare. While we purposely do not include any tracking scripts or analytics of our own on this website, these hosting platforms automatically collect basic diagnostic, network connection, and analytics information (such as IP addresses and browser types) to maintain their infrastructure security and performance. Please refer to their respective privacy policies for details.

4. Local Storage

The personalized Web App controller (accessed via your unique /my/ URL) utilizes your browser's Local Storage to save UI states (like Signal Presets). This operates entirely offline relative to any centralized "Linked Lamp Server" because there is no such server. It talks directly to your third-party MQTT broker.

5. Open Source Transparency

The source code for the Linked Lamp firmware and web interface is completely open and public under the GNU General Public License v3.0 (GPLv3). You are encouraged to read the code, modify it, and audit it for your own security requirements.
