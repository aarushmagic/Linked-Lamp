Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Linked Lamp.

You'll need a few common components for each lamp:

1. An ESP32 development board (the brain).
2. A TTP223 capacitive touch sensor module.
3. A 3D-printed enclosure (STLs provided).
4. Some wire and a 5V USB power supply.

Then you have two choices for the lights:
Option A: The custom Linked Lamp PCB + Through-hole RGB Common Anode LEDs.
Option B: A 16-LED WS2812B NeoPixel Ring.

Because everything is open-source, you can source these parts yourself for just a few dollars each!

Absolutely! We added support for WS2812B individually addressable LED rings. A 16-LED ring is the perfect size for the 3D printed shell. Just connect its Data In pin to GPIO 27 on the ESP32, and select the "NeoPixel Ring" option during the setup wizard to flash the correct firmware.

If you used the one-click browser flash (Chrome/Edge), the config is sent automatically after flashing — check the log output for a "✓ Done" confirmation. If it shows a warning, try re-flashing.

If you used PlatformIO manually, make sure you ran "Upload Filesystem Image" in addition to the regular Upload. This pushes your data/config.json into the ESP32's storage. Once that's done, restart the lamp and it will connect!

Because you are building and configuring your own lamp, updates are completely under your control via OTA (Over-The-Air) updates.

If we release a new feature or bug fix on our GitHub, you can navigate to your personalized Web App (`/my/` link), go to Settings, and tap "Check for Update". Your lamp will automatically download the latest firmware.bin or firmware-neo.bin (depending on your hardware) from our public repository and restart itself—no plugging back into a computer required!

The ESP32 microcontroller only supports 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi networks. It will not connect to a 5 GHz-only network. Fortunately, almost all modern routers dual-broadcast both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands, meaning as long as your router supports legacy devices, your Linked Lamp will connect perfectly fine.
